In statistics, the odds ratio (usually abbreviated "OR") is one of three main ways to quantify how strongly the presence or absence of property A is associated with the presence or absence of property B in a given population. If each individual in a population either does or does not have a property "A", (e.g. "high blood pressure"), and also either does or does not have a property "B" (e.g. "moderate alcohol consumption") where both properties are appropriately defined, then a ratio can be formed which quantitatively describes the association between the presence/absence of "A" (high blood pressure) and the presence/absence of "B" (moderate alcohol consumption) for individuals in the population.

#Slide 2

This ratio is the odds ratio (OR) and can be computed following these steps:

  1. For a given individual that has "B" compute the odds that the same individual has "A"
  2. For a given individual that does not have "B" compute the odds that the same individual has "A"
  3. Divide the odds from step 1 by the odds from step 2 to obtain the odds ratio (OR).

In this app we will compute the OR for the conditions given by the user.

The user has to submit four numbers. First, number of individuals which have property A and property B. Second, number of individuals with property A but not property B. Third, number of individuals without property A but have property B. And finally, those individuals which does not have property A neither property B.

The app will show the table created by the user and the result of the OR calculated.

If the OR is greater than 1, then having "A" is considered to be "associated" with having "B" in the sense that the having of "B" raises (relative to not-having "B") the odds of having "A".

On the other hand, if the OR is less than 1, then having "B" is decrease (relative to not-having "B") the odds of having "A".

If the OR is equal to 1, there is no association between the variables.
